The Visionary Reformer

Mahatma Jyotirao Govindrao Phule (11 April 1827 â€“ 28 November 1890) was an Indian social activist, thinker, anti-caste social reformer and writer from Maharashtra. His work extended to many fields, including eradication of untouchability and the caste system, and women's emancipation.

He and his wife, Savitribai Phule, were pioneers of women's education in India. He led the movement against the prevailing caste-restrictions in India. He revolted against the domination of the Brahmins and struggled for the rights of peasants and other low-caste people.

Early Life & Education

Jyotirao Phule was born in 1827 into a family that belonged to the Mali caste, traditionally occupied as gardeners and considered to be one of the lower castes. His father, Govindrao, was a vegetable vendor. His mother passed away when he was just nine months old.

Despite facing social discrimination, Phule was fortunate to receive an education. He attended the Scottish Mission High School in Pune, where he was influenced by Thomas Paine's ideas on human rights. It was during this time that a life-changing incident occurred - he was humiliated at a Brahmin friend's wedding procession because of his lower caste status. This experience deeply affected him and sparked his lifelong mission to fight against social inequality.

Life Journey

1827

Birth

Born on April 11, 1827, in Satara district of Maharashtra to Govindrao and Chimana Bai.

1840-1847

Education

Attended the Scottish Mission High School in Pune, where he was influenced by Western thoughts and ideas.

1848

Marriage

Married Savitribai Phule, who later became his partner in his social reform movements.

1848

First School for Girls

Opened the first indigenous girls' school in Pune, a revolutionary step in those times.

1851

School for Untouchables

Established a school for children from the untouchable castes, challenging the prevailing social norms.

1873

Satyashodhak Samaj

Founded the Satyashodhak Samaj (Society of Seekers of Truth) to attain equal rights for people from lower castes.

1876-1882

Literary Works

Published several works including "Gulamgiri" (Slavery) and "Shetkaryacha Asud" (Cultivator's Whipcord).

1888

Title of Mahatma

Honored with the title of "Mahatma" (Great Soul) by the people of Maharashtra for his selfless service.

1890

Death

Passed away on November 28, 1890, leaving behind a legacy of social reform and equality.

Major Contributions

E

Education for All

Phule was a firm believer in the power of education to bring about social change. He opened the first indigenous girls' school in Pune in 1848, a revolutionary step in those times.

W

Women's Emancipation

Along with his wife Savitribai, he pioneered women's education in India and fought against practices like child marriage and sati.

C

Caste Equality

He challenged the caste system and worked tirelessly for the rights of the lower castes, establishing the Satyashodhak Samaj in 1873.

F

Farmers' Rights

Through his work "Shetkaryacha Asud," he highlighted the plight of farmers and advocated for their rights against exploitation.

O

Orphanage

Established an orphanage to provide shelter to widows and prevent infanticide, showing his compassion for the marginalized.

L

Literary Works

Authored several books including "Gulamgiri" (Slavery) which compared the plight of lower castes with that of American slaves.

Enduring Legacy

Mahatma Jyotirao Phule's legacy continues to inspire generations of social reformers and activists in India. His pioneering work in education, women's rights, and social equality laid the foundation for many progressive movements that followed.

His ideas influenced many prominent leaders including Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, who acknowledged Phule as one of his three gurus. Today, numerous institutions, universities, and public spaces across India bear his name, honoring his contributions to society.
